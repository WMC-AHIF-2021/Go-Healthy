Booty Exercises

Glute Bridge

  1. Lie on your back, bent your knees and put your feet hip width apart. Put your arms at ypur sides with palms flat ont hte ground.
  2. Tighten your abdominal and gluteal muscles, press your feet into the floor and lift your hips off the floor. Your body should form a straight line from your shoulders to your knees.
  3. Hold it for around 5 seconds.
  4. Slowly lower the starting position and repeat it for around 20 times.

This exercises is good for beginners.

Jumping Squats

  1. Stand in a squat position. Make sure your feets are slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Put your arms at your sides.
  2. Lower your body until your thighs are parallel to your knees. As you squat, move your arms out in front of you, palms together.
  3. Jump off the ground. Try to push your feet as high as possible. You can extend your arms.
  4. Squat back down and repeat it for around 20 times.

If you have problems with your knees or ankles, consult a physical therapist before trying jump squats!

If it's to difficult for you at the beginning, try normal squats (without jumping).

Donkey Kick Backs

  1. Start on all fours. Put your feet hip-width apart and hands under your shoulder. Make sure your neck and spin are neutral.
  2. Begin to lift your rigth leg with your knees bent until your rigth thigh is parallel to the ground. Use your glute to press your foot.
  3. Return back to the startin position and lift your left leg.
  4. Repeat this exercise for around 20 times.

This exercise is perfect for beginners. As an increase, lift your leg straight up!

Walking Lunges

  1. Start standing with your feet shoulder width apart.
  2. Step with the right foot forward, put the weight into your heel.
  3. Bend your right knee and lowering down until it is parallel to the floor. Pause for a beat.
  4. Without moving your rigth leg, move your left foot forward and repeat the same movement.
  5. Perform this for around 20 times on each leg.

If you want a little challenge, hold a pair of weights (you can also use bottles filled with water) at shoulder height, elbows bent in front of body.

Rainbows

  1. First of all, get down on all fours. Straighten your left leg by pulling the toe away from you.
  2. Take your left leg to the left side as far as possible and touch the floor with your toe.
  3. Move your leg on the right side and make sure, that your are touching the ground.
  4. Imagine your are drawing a rainbow with your toes!
  5. Do it again for around 20 times on each side.

You can also do this on your elbows.

Side Lying Leg Raises

  1. Lie down on your right side on a mat or the floor. Put your feet on top of each other. Make sure your body is in a straight line!
  2. Place your left hand for extra support in front of you or let it rest on your leg or hip. Place the other hand under your head.
  3. Raise your left leg as high as possible. You have to stop, if you you feel the muscles flex in your lower back or obliques.
  4. Inhale and lower your leg back down to the right leg.
  5. Repeat this for around 15 times, then switch to the other side.

Hip Abduction

  1. Stand tall. You can hold on something with your hand for support!
  2. Lift one leg away from your body, with your toes pointed forward. Not up!
  3. Start the movement from your hips, not by leaning your torso to the side.
  4. You can see, if it helps to feel your glutes working more, when you are moving your leg a little bit backward or leaning slightly forward.
  5. Do it for around 20 times, then change on the other side!

This is a great exercise for extra work on your side glutes!

Cursty Lunges

  1. Start standing tall with your hip-width apart.
  2. Take a step back to the side with your right leg, so that it crosses behind your front leg, while your hips are still pointing forward.
  3. Make sure, that you are keeping the weight mainly on the front leg!
  4. When your are getting back, push the heel of the left foot to the ground.
  5. Repeat it for around 20 times on each leg!
