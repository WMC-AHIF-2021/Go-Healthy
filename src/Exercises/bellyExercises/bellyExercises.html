Belly Exercises

Sit Up

  1. Start by lying on your back with your knees bent.
  2. Put your fingertips on the back of your ears.
  3. Lift your torso up as close to your thighs as possible.
  4. Lower your torso down to the floor so you are back in the starting position.
  5. Do 3 sets of 10-15 reps.

Sit-ups are carried out in a lying position, so you should choose a flat and as smooth as possible surface, which is horizontal.

Russian Twist

  1. Place a mat or towel on the floor. Elevate your upper body.
  2. Twist. With your arms extended in front of you, twist your torso to the right side. Twist until your arms are parallel to the floor. Hold the position for a second or two and breathe out.
  3. Try to do 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

If it's too hard for you, you can rest your feet on the ground to make it substantially easier.

Crunches

  1. Lie on your back on an exercise mat.
  2. Bend your knees so your feet are flat on the floor.
  3. Cross your arms behind your head.
  4. Lift your shoulder blades off of the mat with a smooth, controlled motion.
  5. If the exercise is too easy pull your legs to your chest at the same time.
  6. Do 5 sets 45 seconds

The crunch, is one of the most popular abdominal muscle exercises and is very effective for strengthening your straight abdominal muscles.

Hollow Hold

  1. Lie on your back, with your arms and legs straight out.
  2. Crunch your abdominal muscles inward, as if you were trying to suck your belly button to the floor.
  3. Raise your arms, legs, and upper back off the floor, creating a “smiley face” with your body.
  4. If the movement is to difficult or you start to feel it in your lower back, bend the legs to make it easier.
  5. Do 3 sets 20 - 30 seconds

Don't allow your lower back to arch off of the floor. This is called lordosis, and it can add strain to your lumbar vertebrae.

Dolphin Plank

  1. Get on your hands and knees.
  2. Raise into dolphin pose.
  3. Walk your feet backward.
  4. Breath through the position.
  5. Do 3 sets of 20 reps. Take a 30-60 second break before moving on to the next exercise.

For beginners relieve neck tension by resting your forehead on a block set between your forearms.

Cat Cow

  1. Begin on your hands and knees in table pose, with a neutral spine. As you inhale and move into cow pose, lift your sit bones upward, press your chest forward and allow your belly to sink.
  2. Lift your head, relax your shoulders away from your ears, and gaze straight ahead.
  3. As you exhale, come into cat pose while rounding your spine outward, tucking in your tailbone, and drawing your pubic bone forward.
  4. Release your head toward the floor — just do't force your chin to your chest. Most importantly, just relax.
  5. You can make this exercise as much as you want it's only good for you.

This exercise is good when you start your training.
